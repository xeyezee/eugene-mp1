Beef Pares

Beef pares is a tantalizing Filipino dish that combine succulent beef chunks and aromatic spices, resulting in a deightful and flavorful culinary experience. The dish features tender cuts of beef simmered in a rich soy sauce and star anise-infused broth, rendering the meat incredibly tender and infused with a delectable savory taste. The beef complimented by a generous serving of garlic fried rice adding fragrant and satisfying component to the meal. Topped with thinly sliced green onions and accompanied by a side i=of sweet spicy soy-based dipping sauce, beef pares is a harmonious blend of textures and flavors that will surely tantalize your taste buds. Whether enjoyed for lunch or dinner, this hearty and comforting dish is a true Filipino classic that never fails to satisfy.

The origin of the term Pares is credited to the carinderia Jonas established by Lolita Tiu and Roger Tiu in 1979 near the then named Calle Retiro (present-day N.S. Amoranto Street).[2][3] The term literally means pairs in English and comes from the practice of "pairing" the beef ulam or dish with garlic fried rice and a light beef broth soup, forming a complete meal.

Tapsilog

Tapsilog is a popular Filipino breakfast dish consist of three main components: tap , sinangag, and itlog, which mean cured beef, garlic fried rice, and fried egg, respectively. The name "tapsilog" is derived from combining the first syllables of these three ingredients. The tapa in tapsilog refers to thinly sliced or shredded beef that has been marinated in a flavorful mixture typically consisting of soy sauce , calamansi juice (Filipino lime), garlic, sugar, and various spices. The beef is often tenderized and left to marinate overnight, allowing the flavors to penetrate the meat. It is then pan-fried or grilled until it's tender and slightly caramelized. Sinangag, the second component, is garlic fried rice. Cooked rice is stir-fried wuth minced garlic until grains become slightly toasted and infused with the aroma of garlic. This step adds a savory and aromatic element to the dish. The final component of a tapsilog is itlog, which is a fried egg.It is usually prepared sunny-side up or over-easy, with the yolk still slightly runny. The fried egg adds a creamy and rich element to the dish, complementing the flavors of a Tapa and sinangag.
